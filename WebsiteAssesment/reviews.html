Slipknot 2025 kit

This is the drumkit of Eloy Casagrande, the former drummer of the Brazilian metal band Sepultura and current drummer of nu-metal band slipknot. It is a custom-made kit hand picked by eloy and it consists of Tama Starclassic Bubinga drums, Paiste cymbals, Evans drumheads, Pro-Mark drumsticks, and Gibraltar hardware. The kit features four bass drums two 22"x16" and two 24"x14, three rack toms 10"x7", 12"x8", and 13"x9", two floor toms 6"x14" and 18"x16", a snare drum 14"x6" (PBB146), and a side snare 14"x6.5" (BB156). On top of all this he has a absolutely stacked amount of cymbals including a Formula 602 Modern Essentials Ride, a Formula 602 Modern Essentials Crash, a RUDE Blast China, a PST X Swiss Flanger Stack, two more Formula 602 Modern Essentials Crashes, and the Paiste 15" Formula 602 Modern Essentials Hi-Hat. This isnt even all of his cymbals, his full kit contains accents and splash cymbals as well. Behind the kit is a 40" gong. It is a truly impressive kit that is a testament to Eloy's skill and dedication as a drummer. the estimated value ranges between AUD $41,250 - $57,750

Dream Monster

This is the drumkit of Mike Portnoy, The current drummer for progressive metal band Dream Theater and former drummer of the band Avenged Sevenfold. It is one of the most iconic and expansive kits that has ever been used by a drummer. Its sheer size and complexity is a testament to Mike's skill and dedication as a drummer. Due to its wide variety of drums and cymbals, it is a versatile kit that can be used for a wide range of musical styles. Attempting to list all of the drums and cymbals on the kit is a fruitless endevour but its most notable features are 3 bass drums, 3 snares and just a ridiculous amount of cymbals, racks and floors. It is known as the Dream Monster and is truly one of the greatest drumkits of all time. It was built by the drumeo crew and contains multiple custom shells heads and 3 custom drum skins. The ergonomics of the kit are also impressive considering its size, with the build being a true testament to the skill of the drumeo crew. the estimated value ranges between AUD $49,500 - $66,000

Tool Fear Inoculum Tour 2024 Kit

This is the drumkit of Danny Carrey the current drummer of the progressive metal band Tool. Being a Paiste featured artist, Danny Carey's cymbal setup includes 5½", 6", and 6½" Paiste 2002 Cup Chimes, along with 8" and 10" Signature Dark Energy Splash Mk I and an 8" Signature Splash. For crashes, he uses two 18" and one 20" Signature Power Crashes. His chinas include an 18" 2002 China, a custom 22" 2002 Novo China, a discontinued 22" Signature Heavy China, and an 11" Noise Works Dark Buzz China Top. Rounding out the setup is a 22" Signature Dry Heavy Ride “Monad” and a 14" Formula 602 Classic Sound Edge Hi-Hat. On top of this, he also has seven electronic drum pads with 3 triggers each and a Roland HandSonic HPD-20. Both the electronic pads and the HandSonic are used to trigger sounds custom made for Danny using Korg Wavedrum and Marimba Lumina. The kit is known for its unique sound and complex rhythms, which is perfect for the progressive music that Danny plays. His playing is incredibly unique and complex, and only a kit a carefully thought out and designed as this could keep up with his skill. It is easlily one of the best drumkits in the world for one of the best drummers in the world. the estimated value ranges between AUD $66,000 - $82,500.

DW R40 Drum Kit

This is the legendary DW R40 drum kit of Late Great Neil Peart, the iconic drummer of Rush. Crafted from rare 1,500-year-old Romanian River Oak, this custom kit was designed in close collaboration with DW to celebrate Rush's 40th anniversary. Featuring intricate wood inlays and premium gold-plated hardware, it's as much a work of art as it is a powerful instrument. Known for its rich, warm tone and exceptional craftsmanship, the R40 perfectly suited Neil's precise and dynamic playing style. This one-of-a-kind setup not only honors his legacy but also stands as one of the most unique and valuable drum kits in the world. Due to the ancient relic wood used in its construction, the kit is likely one of the most expensive in the world, the drum kit alone likely costs around AUD $50,000 and the river oak likely cost upwards of AUD $40,000. The total estimated price is likely above $100,00
